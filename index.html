I work on modelling molecular systems, with a focus on materials, polymers, and large-scale interactions. During my PhD at the University of Luxembourg, I studied how structure and interactions at the molecular level translate into observable properties such as mechanical behaviour and transport.

My work is very hands-on. I build simulations, analyse results, and try to understand what is actually happening in the system. I have worked with tools such as LAMMPS, GROMACS, and custom code, and developed machine learning approaches to estimate interaction energies and forces when standard methods become too costly.

Over time, what became important to me is not just running simulations, but making sense of them. Understanding why a system behaves the way it does, and how changes at a small scale impact the overall behaviour. I have worked on polymers, carbon-based systems, and other complex molecular structures, often in collaboration with other groups to connect modelling results with more practical questions.

My path has taken me through Strasbourg, Milan, Paris, and Luxembourg. I was not the fastest at the beginning, but I learned by observing, asking questions, and building my own way of working. During my PhD, I worked in a large research group where I was exposed to many different approaches. This is where I developed a strong method and became very efficient in my work.

I am now looking to move closer to applied R&D, where this type of work connects directly to real materials, processes, and products.
