About Me

I’m a researcher passionate about the intersection of theoretical chemistry, machine learning, and scientific software development. Currently, I am pursuing a PhD at the University of Luxembourg within the Theoretical Chemical Physics group led by Prof. Alexandre Tkatchenko.

My academic journey has taken me across Europe — from Strasbourg to Milan to Paris — where I earned a double degree Master’s in In Silico Drug Design, blending bioinformatics, quantum chemistry, and data science.

As a PhD researcher, I’ve developed machine learning models to enhance molecular simulations, contributed to international collaborations such as with Google Research – Brain, and taught Python and machine learning courses at the university.

My research spans building GNNs and GANs for chemical property prediction, designing custom scientific software, and working hands-on with Fortran and PyTorch. I'm driven by a curiosity for how computational methods can advance fundamental science.

Beyond research, I’m committed to open-source development, cross-disciplinary teamwork, and communicating complex ideas clearly and accessibly. This site is where I share my work, insights, and ongoing projects.

You can contact me at nils.davoine@uni.lu for more information!
