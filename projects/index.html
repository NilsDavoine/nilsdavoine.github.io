Project 1: Design and Development of an Anti-Inflammatory Drug

Study of covalent bond formation in ligand-protein complexes for various drugs. Our findings unveiled insights into drug-protein affinity and uncovered an unexpected bond formation mechanism, offering novel perspectives for drug design.

Project 2: Predicting Transplacental Drug Passage with Machine Learning.

Employing diverse machine learning models, this project accurately anticipated transplacental drug movement. The insights gained provide valuable tools for evaluating drug administration outcomes. Importantly, our major accomplishment emerged with a neural network model that demonstrated an impressive 95% accuracy in categorizing this complex process.

Learn more about that project here.

Project 3: Exploration of Self-Assembled Structures on Graphene Layers.

In this study, I examined the self-assembled structures formed on a graphene layer using a collection of small molecules exhibiting various types of favored interactions. The investigation involved analyzing both experimental data obtained through Scanning Tunneling Microscopy (STM) and utilizing simulations to compute essential properties such as chemical potential and energy per surface unit. This approach offered comprehensive insights into the assembly behavior and energetics of the molecules on the graphene surface.

Learn more about that project here.

Project 4: Binding Free Energy Calculations

Our project delved into the world of binding free energy calculations. We took a closer look at how different small molecules interact within a host-guest system, measuring the work required for these interactions. This study provided essential insights, allowing us to compare binding free energy values among a variety of small molecules with unique characteristics.
