Interaction Modelling

Machine learning for interaction energies and forces in molecular systems.

Data-Driven Modelling of Molecular Interactions

This work focused on improving how interaction energies and forces are estimated in molecular simulations. In many systems, especially large ones such as polymers or extended molecular structures, accurate calculations quickly become too expensive. The goal was to develop alternative approaches that remain reliable while reducing computational cost.

Context

Accurate modelling of interactions is central to understanding material behaviour. Methods based on quantum calculations are precise, but scale poorly with system size. This creates a gap between accuracy and practical usability when working on large molecular systems.

My Work

I developed machine learning models to estimate interaction energies and forces based on molecular configurations. These models were trained on reference data and designed to reproduce physical behaviour while remaining computationally efficient.

The work included building datasets, designing model architectures, training and validating models, and analysing their behaviour. A strong focus was placed on understanding when the models work well, and where their limits are.

Technical Focus

• Modelling interaction energies and forces in molecular systems
• Machine learning using PyTorch and TensorFlow
• Dataset construction from simulation data
• Model validation and physical consistency checks
• Application to polymers, carbon chains, and large systems

Outcome

The project led to models capable of approximating interaction energies with significantly reduced computational cost. This made it possible to study larger systems or longer simulation times while keeping a reasonable level of accuracy.

Relevance

This type of approach is useful in materials modelling, where simulations need to balance accuracy and scale. It reflects my general way of working: combining physical understanding with data-driven methods to make complex systems more tractable.
