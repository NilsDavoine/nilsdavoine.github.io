High-Performance Simulation Tools for Molecular Systems

This work was developed during my PhD in theoretical chemical physics. The goal was to build and use simulation tools to study large molecular systems, while ensuring reliability, performance, and usability in a research environment.

Context

When working with polymers, carbon chains, or extended molecular systems, simulations quickly become expensive and complex. Accurately capturing interactions, especially long-range effects, requires both careful modelling and efficient computation.

My Work

I worked with simulation tools such as LAMMPS, GROMACS, and custom implementations. In parallel, I developed Fortran-based tools to support analysis and simulation workflows. The focus was on making simulations stable, reproducible, and efficient for large systems.

Technical Focus

• Simulation of polymers, carbon-based systems, and molecular structures
• Development of scientific software (Fortran, Python)
• Analysis of interaction energies and forces
• Workflow design for reproducibility and validation
• Performance and numerical stability improvements

Why It Matters

A simulation is only useful if it can be trusted and reused. This work focused on building tools that are not only technically correct, but also clear and usable by others. It shaped the way I approach technical problems today.

Relevance

This experience is directly connected to materials modelling and R&D work. It trained me to understand interactions at the molecular level, and how these interactions translate into system behaviour.
